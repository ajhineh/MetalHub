# Glossary

This document outlines the core terminology that defines the conceptual model of MetalHub.

**Digital Case**
Every project, RFQ, or order in MetalHub is treated as a Digital Case. It acts as the central hub of truth, containing all communications, documents, decisions, and history. Ultimately, the Digital Case evolves into the "Digital Twin of the Project."

**Industrial Memory (Collective Memory)**
The platform's capability to learn from past executions, capturing technical, commercial, and operational realities so that every future decision is informed by historical evidence.

**Decision Capital**
The cumulative value derived from past judgments and structured data. It represents the platform's capacity to convert raw data into actionable, high-quality guidance for the user.

**Uncertainty Cost Index (UCI)**
A metric designed to measure the hidden costs of uncertainty in a project, including delays in communication, missing documentation, and unrecorded changes. 

**Guidance**
Adaptive intelligence that assists users in navigating complex decisions. Guidance does not make the decision for the user, but rather illuminates the best paths and highlights risks based on evidence.

**Single Source of Truth**
A core architectural principle ensuring that all stakeholders (buyers, suppliers, engineers) are looking at the exact same data, documents, and status at any given time, eliminating version conflicts and miscommunications.
