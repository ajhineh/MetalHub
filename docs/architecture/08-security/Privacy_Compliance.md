# Privacy & Compliance

## 1. GDPR Compliance (General Data Protection Regulation)
All operations within the EU must strictly adhere to GDPR principles:
- **Data Minimization**: Collect only the data necessary for the RFQ or contract.
- **Right to be Forgotten**: Buyers and Suppliers can request full deletion of personal identifying information (PII). Note: Industrial Evidence Data (anonymized performance metrics) remains in the Knowledge Graph for collective learning, stripped of PII.
- **Consent**: Explicit consent required before recording Whisper transcripts via VoIP.

## 2. CBAM (Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism)
As a platform dealing with heavy industrial manufacturing (Steel Structures) imported into the EU:
- MetalHub requires suppliers to upload verifiable Carbon Emission certificates during the RFQ process.
- The Decision Engine calculates carbon footprint estimates and flags non-compliant offers before presenting them to the Buyer.

## 3. Sanctions Compliance (Iran-Europe Supply Chain)
Due to the hybrid outsourced fabrication model potentially involving the Iran supply chain:
- **KYC/KYB Integration**: All suppliers must pass a strict Know Your Business (KYB) check against international sanction lists (OFAC, EU Sanctions Map) before onboarding.
- **Financial Firewalls**: No direct monetary transactions flow through sanctioned gateways. MetalHub acts strictly as a technological matching and QA evaluation platform in these contexts, leaving financial execution to compliant third-party legal entities or alternative legal routing as defined in the contract.
- **Dual-Use Goods Verification**: The RFQ processing engine flags any CAD drawings or material specs that fall under Dual-Use export control regulations.

## 1. Data Is the Primary Asset (PRN-014)
Data entrusted to MetalHub is our most valuable asset. Protecting it is paramount to maintaining the health of the industrial ecosystem.

## 2. Compliance Framework
- **Regulatory Alignment**: The platform must adhere to global and regional data protection regulations (e.g., GDPR, CCPA).
- **Industrial Standards**: Compliance with specific industrial data handling and confidentiality requirements.

## 3. Privacy by Design
- **Data Minimization**: Only collect data that directly contributes to the workflow or knowledge compounding.
- **Secure Tenancy**: Strict logical separation of tenant data to ensure proprietary industrial knowledge is not leaked.

## 4. Transparency
While transparency builds trust (PRN-013), it must be balanced with privacy. Only evidence that is permitted to be shared according to the platform's principles and user agreements will be visible across the network.
