## Anti-Principles
- **APR-001**: MetalHub does not replace humans. [Source: Architecture Consolidation]
- **APR-002**: Data accumulation without refinement is not knowledge. [Source: Architecture Consolidation]
- **APR-003**: Guidance must not lead to user dependency. [Source: Architecture Consolidation]
- **APR-004**: Trust must never be gained through obfuscation or exaggeration. [Source: Architecture Consolidation]
- **APR-005**: Collaboration Without Dependence - Collaboration must not create unhealthy dependencies. [Source: Volume X, Chapter 4.7]

# 02-Constraints

## Architectural Constraints
- **CON-001**: Avoid "Feature Creep" - Every feature must map to a reduction in uncertainty or an increase in organizational wisdom. [Source: Architecture Consolidation]
- **CON-002**: Avoid "Knowledge Debt" - Knowledge must not be lost or untraceable. [Source: Architecture Consolidation]
- **CON-003**: No Unverified Claims - The system is built on an event-driven "Commitment" model where every stage is a verifiable event (evidence-based). [Source: Architecture Consolidation]
- **CON-004**: Decision Traceability - Every commit/action must be linked back to a Mission/ADR/Capability. [Source: Architecture Consolidation]
- **CON-005**: Transparency Over Control - Governance is distributed and evidence-based. [Source: Architecture Consolidation]

# SRS Traceability Audit: Functional Use Cases, Capabilities, Pre-conditions, and Success Scenarios

## 1. Use Cases (UC)

### From Architecture Consolidation
* **UC-001**: Review and consolidate MetalHub vocabulary (e.g., Digital Case, Industrial Memory, Decision Intelligence) to ensure single definitions. [Source: Architecture Consolidation]
* **UC-002**: Extract, review, and align Core Principles to ensure no conflicts exist. [Source: Architecture Consolidation]
* **UC-003**: Conduct an Architecture Integrity Review to check for duplicate ADRs, SIs, and Capabilities across volumes. [Source: Architecture Consolidation]
* **UC-004**: Evaluate Narrative Consistency to ensure a cohesive story from Volume I to Volume IX. [Source: Architecture Consolidation]
* **UC-005**: Assess Future Readiness by checking if the architecture will remain valid ten years from now. [Source: Architecture Consolidation]
* **UC-006**: Define Impact Architecture (Volume X) outlining the long-term societal and industrial impact. [Source: Architecture Consolidation]
* **UC-007**: Define Anti-Principles alongside Principles to establish clear architectural and product boundaries. [Source: Architecture Consolidation]

### From Volume III
* **UC-008: Opportunity Intake** - The system must allow users to input initial project needs via online forms, emails, CAD files, or other means to generate an initial requirement. [Source: Volume III]
* **UC-009: Engineering Validation** - The system must allow engineering review of drawings, specs, and manufacturability before generating a quotation. [Source: Volume III]
* **UC-010: Capability Planning & Matching** - The system must identify required production capabilities and recommend the best providers using a Capability Graph. [Source: Volume III]
* **UC-011: Distributed RFQ Generation** - The system must send targeted RFQs to selected providers, ensuring each provider only sees the portion relevant to them. [Source: Volume III]
* **UC-012: Commercial Consolidation** - The system must aggregate received quotations, detect inconsistencies, specify risks, and prepare a final proposal. [Source: Volume III]
* **UC-013: Project Kick-off** - Upon approval, the system must auto-generate a Project Plan, milestones, responsibilities, schedules, and a Risk Register. [Source: Volume III]
* **UC-014: Production Execution & Monitoring** - The system must capture images, videos, progress reports, and QC evidence from Capability Providers during execution. [Source: Volume III]
* **UC-015: Customer Tracking** - Customers must be able to track project status, timelines, documents, and ETAs via a tracking code without complex logins. [Source: Volume III]
* **UC-016: Quality & Acceptance** - The system must review QC reports, dimensions, NCRs, and approvals before authorizing shipment. [Source: Volume III]
* **UC-017: Logistics Coordination** - The system must plan transport methods, loading/unloading requirements, incoterms, insurance, and export documents. [Source: Volume III]
* **UC-018: Knowledge Capture & Trust Update** - Post-delivery, the system must extract lessons learned, update supplier trust scores based on actual performance, and version the knowledge. [Source: Volume III]

# Quality Attributes (SRS)

## Architecture Consolidation
* **QA-401**: **Human Empowerment (Human Ownership)**: The system shall be strictly designed to empower human users and must never act to replace human presence or judgment. `[Source: Architecture Consolidation]`
* **QA-402**: **Trustability (Trust Architecture)**: Trust must be inherently designed into the system's core; it must never be acquired through information concealment or exaggeration. `[Source: Architecture Consolidation]`
* **QA-403**: **Autonomy (Anti-Dependency)**: The system's adaptive guidance must be calibrated to foster user competence without ever leading to user dependency on the system. `[Source: Architecture Consolidation]`
* **QA-404**: **Defendability**: The system's operations and recommendations must remain logically and procedurally defendable at all times. `[Source: Architecture Consolidation]`
* **QA-405**: **Maturation Focus**: The ultimate impact of the system's execution must drive measurable growth and maturity in users, organizational structures, and industrial collaboration over time. `[Source: Architecture Consolidation]`

## Volume IV - Platform Architecture
* **QA-001 (Maintainability):** The system shall ensure Domains are independently developable with minimal dependencies, ensuring that each Domain is an independent owner of its capabilities and data to prevent monolithic entanglement. `[Source: Volume IV, Chapter 1]`
* **QA-002 (Security & Access Control):** The system shall include Platform Services specifically for Identity Management, Access Control, and API Gateway traffic routing to safeguard platform data. `[Source: Volume IV, Chapter 2]`
* **QA-003 (Reliability / Trust):** The system shall objectively measure Trust rather than assume it, dynamically evaluating actor performance through SLA Fulfillment, Commitment Tracking, and Historical Reliability scoring. `[Source: Volume IV, Chapter 2]`
* **QA-004 (Auditability):** The system shall ensure no operational decisions are made without verifiable Evidence, utilizing Digital Signatures, Version History, and rigorous change tracking (including reason for change and approver). `[Source: Volume IV, Chapter 4]`
* **QA-005 (Transparency):** The system shall provide complete visibility of object lifecycles, maintaining a clear Revision History for engineering maps, BOMs, and contracts to prevent ambiguity across the ecosystem. `[Source: Volume IV, Chapter 4]`

## Volume VI - System Architecture
* **QA-101**: **Modularity / Replaceability** - Every component must be completely replaceable without breaking other components in the system architecture. `[Source: Volume VI]`
* **QA-102**: **Observability** - Every component and interaction must be designed to be observable and measurable from day one, not added as an afterthought. `[Source: Volume VI]`
* **QA-103**: **Explainability** - All decisions and outputs, whether generated by AI models, algorithms, or workflows, must be fully explainable. `[Source: Volume VI]`
* **QA-104**: **Traceability** - All critical interactions, business events, and decisions must be fully traceable across the Decision Spine. `[Source: Volume VI]`
* **QA-105**: **Composability** - Top-level system components must be composable into independent vertical products (e.g., MetalHub RFQ, MetalHub Engineering) sharing the same core. `[Source: Volume VI]`
* **QA-106**: **Maintainability** - The architecture must establish and continuously measure "Business Architecture Debt" to prevent structural decay over time. `[Source: Volume VI]`
* **QA-107**: **Reliability (Trust)** - Trust must be an embedded architectural layer, ensuring absolute confidence in decisions, identities, and industrial memory preservation. `[Source: Volume VI]`
* **QA-108**: **Contextuality** - Information shall not exist outside of a business context; all information must belong to an explicitly defined business object. `[Source: Volume VI]`

## Volume VII - Execution Architecture
* **QA-201**: **Traceability** - The architecture must ensure an unbroken chain from the initial Customer Intent down to Work Packages, Decisions, and final Evidence. `[Source: Volume VII]`
* **QA-202**: **Transparency** - The system must surface the real-time health, momentum, readiness, and exact physical state of all execution components to authorized stakeholders to build trust. `[Source: Volume VII]`
* **QA-203**: **Explainability** - The system must provide clear context and reasoning for its Readiness Scores, Decision Confidence ratings, and recovery path recommendations. `[Source: Volume VII]`
* **QA-204**: **Accountability** - The system must guarantee that there is no ambiguity regarding the singular ownership of any Work Package, Exception, or Decision. `[Source: Volume VII]`
* **QA-205**: **Adaptability** - The system must gracefully handle extreme uncertainty, permitting execution paths to flex, split, or reorder without breaking the underlying data integrity. `[Source: Volume VII]`
* **QA-206**: **Consistency** - The system must enforce rigorous execution standards via evidence-based gating while remaining flexible enough to manage diverse industrial scenarios. `[Source: Volume VII]`
* **QA-207**: **Learnability (Systemic)** - The platform must inherently absorb data from daily execution (Work DNA, Decision Ledger, Exception Learning) to make the organization structurally smarter over time. `[Source: Volume VII]`

## Volume VIII - Technology Architecture
* **QA-301**: **Vendor Neutrality** - The platform must remain independent of specific technology generations and vendors to prevent lock-in and ensure longevity. `[Source: Volume VIII]`
* **QA-302**: **Explainability** - AI systems must not act as "black boxes"; all insights must be fully explainable and traceable to their context. `[Source: Volume VIII]`
* **QA-303**: **Observability** - The architecture must provide observability at the business platform level, allowing real-time tracking of domain health and pending decisions. `[Source: Volume VIII]`
* **QA-304**: **Resilience** - The architecture must provide Organizational Resilience, ensuring the system can continue operating through external disruptions, tool deprecations, or supplier changes. `[Source: Volume VIII]`
* **QA-305**: **Reversibility** - All architectural and technology changes must be capable of being deployed gradually, tested on new projects, and completely reversed without disrupting ongoing execution. `[Source: Volume VIII]`
* **QA-306**: **Knowledge Retention** - The system must provide Knowledge Resilience, guaranteeing that organizational knowledge and memory persist even if entire teams or vendors change. `[Source: Volume VIII]`
* **QA-307**: **Data Continuity** - The platform must guarantee Digital Continuity, preserving project files and execution states during crises, cloud migrations, or internet outages. `[Source: Volume VIII]`
