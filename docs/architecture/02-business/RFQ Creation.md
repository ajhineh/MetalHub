# RFQ Creation

## Goal
To translate validated engineering needs into clear, distributed requests for quotations that accurately reflect project demands and target the most appropriate supplier capabilities.

## Inputs
- Validated engineering drawings and specifications
- Bill of Materials (BOM) and material requirements
- Project timelines and required delivery dates
- Output from Supplier Discovery (target supplier list)

## Outputs
- Structured, unambiguous RFQ packages
- Targeted distribution to selected capability providers
- Tracking metrics for RFQ views and responses
- Initial Digital Case linkages for traceability

## Actors
- Procurement Manager
- Engineering Reviewer
- Capability Provider (Supplier)

## Rules
- RFQs can only be generated and distributed *after* engineering validation is successfully completed.
- The system must ensure each provider only sees the portion of the RFQ relevant to their specific capabilities (Distributed RFQ Generation).
- All RFQ requirements must be tied to objective evidence to prevent misunderstandings.

## Success Metrics
- RFQ response rate and speed from suppliers
- Accuracy and completeness of quotes received
- Time elapsed from requirement validation to RFQ distribution
- Reduction in supplier clarification requests (RFIs)
